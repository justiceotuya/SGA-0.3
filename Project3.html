Akpu and Egusi Soup
Akpu is a Nigerian dish made from fermented wet paste of cassava. This is done by soaking the cassava for a few days, Egusi, otherwise known as Pumpkin Seed or Melon, is most common in West Africa. Egusi soup on the other hand is a type of soup thickened with ground Egusi i.e. after the dried melon seeds must have been peeled and powdered in a grinding engine (usually a hand-operated one).

Roasted Plantain and Groundnut
Plantain is one of the staples in Ghana. It can be eaten with any stew or soup or taken as snack, when chopped and fried or roasted with groundnuts as accompaniment.

Beans
Nigerian Stewed Beans is a Nigerian Beans recipe made with Honey or Black Eyed Beans. Stewed Beans is quite similar to the popular Beans Pottage, the main difference being the addition of ground fresh pepper.

Yam Porridge
Yam porridge or yam soup as it is sometimes called is mainly yam cooked with ingredients and the resulting dish contains some soupy liquid. Yam porridge is counted among the easy to make Nigerian food. It is a dietary food for breakfast, lunch or dinner.
